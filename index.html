HOW I WORK

Research-driven design with functional validation.

As a current student in SEG3125 UI Design & Development, I'm actively learning evidence-based methodologies from NN/g, visual communication principles, and user-centered design. My design process blends structured research with creative exploration.

My typical workflow: research → create → refine → test → validate. I start by gathering research and inspiration to understand user needs and design direction. I then translate ideas into wireframes before moving into high-fidelity mockups in Figma. From there, I build interactive prototypes, refine the design based on feedback, and test functionality against user requirements.

I'm continuously improving my front-end toolkit: HTML/CSS, Bootstrap 5, JavaScript, React. Through my work on the uOttawa Tennis Club development team, I have strengthened my collaboration skills by working closely with teammates in structured workflows. This experience has also helped me refine my UI/UX design process. As a result, I have become more effective at translating user needs and requirements into practical, well-structured design solutions.
